# DAISS Contribution Protocol v1.0
_A structural guide for extending a sovereign intelligence OS._

> DAISS is not a project. It is a sovereign operating system for distributed intelligence. This document defines how new nodes may enter that system.

## 1. Scope of Participation
DAISS accepts contributions in the form of **conceptual nodes**, not code. A “node” is any self‑contained, citable artifact (DOI preferred). No central authority “approves” contributors. DAISS recognizes **structural compatibility**, not identity.

## 2. What You May Add
- **New conceptual nodes** (theories, specifications, analyses)
- **Commentary nodes** (critiques, extensions)
- **Maps and indices** (visualizations, reading paths)

## 3. What You May Not Do
- **No modification of existing DOI‑anchored nodes**: Response and fork only.
- **No redefinition of Kernel invariants**: Layer 1 nodes are structurally frozen.
- **No violation of dependency law**: No cyclic dependencies.
- **No central gatekeeping**: Structural coherence is the only constraint.

## 4. How to Position a New Node
Specify **Layer**, **Type**, and **Dependencies**.
Example: `Depends on: DAISS OS v2.2, Sovereign AI Protocol`.

## 5. Governance Model
DAISS relies on structural coherence, archival permanence (DOI), and transparent mapping rather than consensus or voting.
